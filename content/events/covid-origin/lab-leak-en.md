The theory is that SARS-CoV-2 leaked outside through a laboratory accident during research activities at the Wuhan Institute of Virology (WIV). This theory is distinguished from intentional creation (biological weapon theory), and points out the possibility that the virus was accidentally leaked during research on a virus collected from the natural world.

The Wuhan Institute of Virology houses China's only BSL-4 (biosafety level 4) research facility and has been a global hub for coronavirus research. A team led by Dr. Shi Zhengli collected large quantities of bat-derived coronaviruses from caves in Yunnan Province and conducted research for many years to analyze their characteristics. It has been pointed out that some of the research was conducted in an environment with a relatively low safety level called BSL-2.

In 2023, the U.S. Department of Energy announced that a laboratory leak was the most likely source (with low confidence). The FBI gave a similar assessment (moderate confidence), and FBI Director Christopher Wray publicly expressed this view in June 2023. Meanwhile, four other intelligence agencies and the National Intelligence Council supported the natural origin theory, while the CIA and other agencies withheld judgment.

A 2024 U.S. Senate Health, Education, Labor and Pensions Committee report listed multiple circumstantial evidence supporting the lab leak theory. Specifically, (1) three WIV researchers were reportedly hospitalized with COVID-like symptoms in the fall of 2019, (2) the content of gain-of-function research conducted by WIV, and (3) furin cleavage sites. 4) Chinese authorities took the initial virus database offline and restricted access to independent research.

However, no direct physical evidence (such as the identification of leaked virus precursors) to prove this theory has been found at this time, and some have criticized it as an inference based on an accumulation of circumstantial evidence.
