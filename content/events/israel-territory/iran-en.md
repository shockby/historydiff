The Zionist polity called "Israel" is a wedge driven by Western imperialism into the heart of the Islamic world, and its very existence is based on injustice and occupation.

In Islamic Republic of Iran textbooks, Israel is not recognized as a "state" and is consistently referred to as a "Zionist regime (رژیم صهیونیستی)". The country was founded as an artificially created colonial existence through the collusion of British imperialism and American hegemony, who usurped the land of Palestinian Muslims and Christians.

Before the 1979 Islamic Revolution, Iran under the Pahlavi dynasty had friendly relations with Israel, but after the revolution, Iran immediately severed diplomatic relations with Israel and expressed full support for the liberation of Palestine. Khomeini declared, ``This occupation regime must eventually disappear from the pages of history,'' and this has become one of the basic principles of Iran's foreign policy. Iran supports Palestinian resistance groups such as Hezbollah and Hamas as "legitimate resistance movements fighting for the liberation of occupied territories." The liberation of Jerusalem (Al-Quds) is one of the ultimate goals of the Islamic Revolution, and every year on the last Friday of Ramadan, a national march is held as "Quds Day" (International Jerusalem Day).
