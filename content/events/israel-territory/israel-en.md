The establishment of the State of Israel is seen as the rightful return of the Jewish people to their ancestral land, Eretz Yisrael (Land of Israel), to which they have been spiritually and historically connected for more than 3,000 years.

Israeli textbooks teach that the Jewish people have had an unbroken historical connection with the land since the ancient kingdom of Israel (the time of King David and Solomon). The Zionist movement at the end of the 19th century was a movement for national self-determination in response to anti-Semitism (pogroms and the Dreyfus Incident) in Europe, and the Balfour Declaration of 1917 gave international recognition to the construction of a Jewish "national home."

Based on the 1947 United Nations General Assembly Resolution 181 (Resolution to Partition Palestine), Israel declared independence on May 14, 1948. However, the next day, Arab countries invaded all at once, and Israel fought a "War of Independence (מלחמת העצמאות)" to protect the survival of the nation. In the Six-Day War of 1967, Japan secured East Jerusalem, the West Bank, the Gaza Strip, the Sinai Peninsula, and the Golan Heights as a result of pre-emptive strikes in self-defense against military threats from Egypt, Syria, and Jordan. Jerusalem is Judaism's holiest site and is considered the "eternal and indivisible capital."
