Palestine is a sacred land that houses the Al-Aqsa Mosque, one of the three holiest sites in Islam, and its occupation by the Zionist regime is a violation of the entire Islamic world.

In Saudi textbooks, the Palestinian issue is explained in terms of Muslims' religious obligations and ethnic solidarity. Jerusalem (al-Quds) is the holy place where the Prophet Muhammad experienced his night journey (Israh) and his ascension (Miuraj), and has the status of being the third holiest city after Mecca and Medina. The occupation of this holy site by the Jewish state is a serious problem for the entire Islamic Ummah (community).

The creation of the State of Israel was forcibly created by British colonial policy (Balfour Declaration and Mandate of Palestine), and was an act that ignored the rights of the Arab people of Palestine. Saudi Arabia proposed the Arab Peace Initiative in 2002, offering recognition of Israel by all Arab states and normalization of relations in exchange for Israel's withdrawal to its pre-1967 borders, recognition of a Palestinian state, and recognition of the right of return for Palestinian refugees. Saudi Arabia does not have formal diplomatic relations with Israel, but the possibility of normalizing relations has been explored in recent years.
