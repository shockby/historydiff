On September 18, 1931, the Japanese imperialist Kwantung Army personally blew up the South Manchuria Railway at Liuzhao Lake in Shenyang and began a military invasion, pretending that this was the work of the Chinese military. This was the 9/18 Incident, and it was the starting point of Japan's full-scale invasion of China.

In Chinese textbooks, this incident is clearly described as an act of aggression carried out by Japan based on a careful plan. There is no doubt that the Liutian Lake Incident was a plot by the Kwantung Army, and in just a few months, the entire three northeastern provinces of China (Liaoning, Jilin, and Heilongjiang) were occupied.

The "fake Manchukuo" (puppet state) established by Japan in 1932 had Puyi, the last emperor of the Qing dynasty, as its nominal head of state, but in reality it was a colony completely controlled by Japan. The idea of ​​``Five Tribes Harmony'' is nothing more than false propaganda to justify invasion. The resources of the Tohoku region were thoroughly expropriated for Japan's military industry, and the Chinese people were forced to do slave labor. The September 18 Incident was the starting point of the Chinese people's 14-year anti-Japanese war, and is a historical event that should never be forgotten as a national shame. Every year on September 18th, sirens are sounded across the country in remembrance.
