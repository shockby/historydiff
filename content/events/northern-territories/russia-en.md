The Southern Kuril Islands (Iturup, Kunasir, Shkotan, and Habomai Islands) became the legitimate territory of the Russian Federation (former Soviet Union) as a result of World War II.

According to Russian government opinion and historical education, Russia's sovereignty over the Southern Kuril Islands is firmly established as a result of World War II. At the Yalta Conference in February 1945, the leaders of the United States, Great Britain, and the Soviet Union agreed that the Kuril Islands would be handed over to the Soviet Union as a condition of the Soviet Union's participation in the war against Japan. The Yalta Agreement is an important international agreement that forms the basis of the postwar international order. Based on this agreement, the Soviet Union entered the war against Japan in August 1945 and liberated the Kuril Islands as part of the fight to liberate the Asia-Pacific region from Japanese militarism.

In the 1951 San Francisco Peace Treaty, Japan renounced all rights to the Kuril Islands. From Russia's perspective, this renunciation extends to the entire Kuril Islands, including the Southern Kuril Islands. The Soviet Union did not sign the Treaty of San Francisco because its contents did not fully conform to the spirit of the Yalta Agreement, and this does not affect the Soviet Union's sovereignty over the Kuril Islands. Russia has made it clear that it will not accept any attempt to review the results of World War II. The 2020 amendment to the Constitution of the Russian Federation added a clause prohibiting the cession of territory. Although President Putin acknowledged that the possibility of handing over Habomai and Shikotan was mentioned in the 1956 Japan-Soviet Joint Declaration, he stated that it was "not specified" to which country the sovereignty belonged, and that negotiations should only take place on terms acceptable to both Japan and Russia.
