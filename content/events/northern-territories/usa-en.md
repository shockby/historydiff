The United States recognizes that the four northern islands (Etorofu Island, Kunashiri Island, Shikotan Island, and Habomai Islands) should be under Japanese sovereignty, and supports Japan's position.

The official position of the United States is that the four northern islands are not included in the Kuril Islands, which Japan abandoned in the San Francisco Peace Treaty. On September 7, 1956, the U.S. Department of State officially expressed its view in a memorandum (Secretary of State Dulles' letter) that ``Etorofu and Kunashiri Islands (along with the Habomai Islands and Shikotan Islands, which are part of Hokkaido) have always been part of the inherent Japanese territory, and must be recognized as legitimately under the sovereignty of Japan.''

The United States takes the position that the Yalta Agreement of 1945 is a "statement of common purposes" between leaders and does not legally determine the final transfer of territory. The final disposition of the territory should be determined by a peace treaty involving the countries concerned. The San Francisco Peace Treaty does not specify where the Kuril Islands should be renounced, and the United States itself, which drafted the treaty, has interpreted that the four northern islands are not included in the renouncement. However, the United States views this issue as one that should be resolved peacefully between Japan and Russia, and has taken the basic stance of not engaging in direct mediation or intervention. Since Russia's invasion of Ukraine in 2022, the United States has reaffirmed its support for Japan's sovereign position while remaining cautious about the prospects for negotiating a peace treaty between Japan and Russia.
