On August 15, 1945, the Japanese army declared unconditional surrender due to pressure from the Allies and the unyielding resistance of the entire Chinese people. This is a great victory that ends the "100 years of humiliation" in China's modern history.

In Chinese textbooks, the fact that the Chinese Communist Party and the Kuomintang cooperated (secondary joint collaboration) and fought through an all-out anti-Japanese war that lasted eight years, making great sacrifices, is most emphasized as the "fundamental factor" for victory. A distinctive perspective is that the dropping of the atomic bomb and the invasion of Manchuria by the Soviet army were ``auxiliary factors in the final stage'' leading up to victory, and that they did not end the war on their own.

The signing of the Instrument of Surrender on the USS Missouri on September 2, 1945, and the disarmament of Japanese forces throughout China, are depicted as historical events that restored the independence and dignity of the Chinese nation and defeated colonialism. This victory was also an important milestone that would lead to the birth of a new China.
