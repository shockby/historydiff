"Sea of ​​Japan" is the only internationally established name, and the Japanese government has consistently maintained its legitimacy.

The name Sea of ​​Japan was spontaneously established in the international community as a result of European exploration and surveying in the early 19th century. The Japanese government refutes the claim that the name spread as a result of Japan's colonial rule and imperialism, saying that this is contrary to fact. According to a survey by the Japanese government, the overwhelming majority of 19th century Western maps used the term "Sea of ​​Japan," and this name had been established even before Korea became a colony.

"Japan Sea" has been used in "Boundaries of Oceans and Seas (S-23)" published by the International Hydrographic Organization (IHO) since its first edition in 1929. In 2020, the IHO decided to transition to a new method of managing S-23 using numerical identifiers, but the Japanese government has stated that this will not affect the existing name of "Sea of ​​Japan." The United Nations has also officially used the term "Sea of ​​Japan," and this policy was confirmed in a 2004 statement by the United Nations Secretariat. The Japanese government says South Korea's claim was first raised at the United Nations Conference on the Standardization of Place Names in 1992, and there is no basis for overturning long-standing international practice.
