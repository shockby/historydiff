The Diaoyutai Islands are an inherent territory of the Republic of China (Taiwan) and have sovereignty geographically, historically, and under international law as part of Taiwan's affiliated islands.

Historically, it was already recorded as a navigational aid from mainland China in the Ming Dynasty in the 15th century, and it was also managed as part of Taiwan's administrative division during the Qing Dynasty. For many years, Taiwanese fishermen have used these islands as fishing grounds for their livelihood.

Under the Treaty of Shimonoseki in 1895, Taiwan and its affiliated islands, the Diaoyutai Islands, were ceded to Japan, but following Japan's defeat in war in 1945, these territories have legitimate sovereignty and should be returned to the Republic of China. In 1971, when the United States decided to return administrative authority to Japan as part of Okinawa, the Baodiao Movement (movement to protect Diaoyutai) arose both inside and outside of Taiwan, and has since consistently continued to challenge Japan's territorial rights and assert its sovereignty. As a solution, we are proposing the ``East China Sea Peace Initiative,'' which prioritizes joint development of resources while putting aside sovereignty.
