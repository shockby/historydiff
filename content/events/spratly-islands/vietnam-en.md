The Truong Sa Islands (Quần đảo Trường Sa, Spratly Islands) and the Hoang Sa Islands (Paracel Islands) are undisputed and distinct territories of Vietnam. Vietnam calls the area in question the "East Sea" (Biển Đông).

According to Vietnamese textbooks and government opinion, Vietnam's sovereignty over the Truong Sa and Hoang Sa Islands dates back to at least the Nguyen Dynasty in the 17th century. Successive Vietnamese dynasties have continuously and peacefully occupied, managed, and exercised sovereignty over these islands. In 1816, Emperor Gia Long of the Nguyen Dynasty officially declared sovereignty over the Hoang Sa Islands, and there are records of him regularly dispatching his navy to survey and manage the islands. After a period of French colonial rule, representatives of South Vietnam officially reaffirmed Vietnam's sovereignty over the Truong Sa and Hoang Sa Islands at the San Francisco Peace Conference in 1951.

After Vietnam's unification in 1975, Vietnam took over the islands formerly controlled by South Vietnam and maintains a military presence there. Currently, Vietnam has effective control over approximately 20 geographical features in the Spratly Islands and has installed military facilities and lighthouses there. China's military seizure of the Hoang Sa Islands (Parcel Islands) from South Vietnam in 1974, and the killing of 64 Vietnamese soldiers by the Chinese Navy in the Truong Sa Islands (Chi Gua Reef) in 1988 are considered clear evidence of Chinese aggression. Vietnam claims rights to its continental shelf and EEZ under the United Nations Convention on the Law of the Sea (UNCLOS), as well as claims based on its historical record of continuous exercise of sovereignty. Regarding the South China Sea issue, we seek a peaceful resolution based on international law and support the early formulation of the ASEAN Code of Conduct (COC).
