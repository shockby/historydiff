In 1982, the Japanese government changed the description of "invasion" into China in history textbooks to "expansion" through textbook certification. This is a systematic attempt by Japan's right-wing forces and government to falsify history.

In Chinese history education and the media, Japan's textbook problem is repeatedly criticized as an expression of "historical revisionism" that is deeply rooted in Japanese society. To describe a war of aggression as an "advancement" is to downplay the sacrifices of tens of millions of Chinese people and to cover up the fact of the invasion. Japanese textbooks have consistently moved in the direction of trivializing war crimes, such as by obscuring the number of victims of the Nanjing Massacre and deleting mention of the comfort women issue.

The Chinese government strongly criticized the textbooks published by the Association for the Creation of New History Textbooks, which passed certification in 2001 and 2005, for their content that justifies and glorifies wars of aggression. China has repeatedly called on Japan to "face up to its past history with sincerity, like Germany, and to sincerely repent and apologize." The textbook issue, along with visits to Yasukuni Shrine and the Senkaku Islands issue, continues to be an important diplomatic issue as a ``historical perception issue'' that touches on the basis of Japan-China relations.
