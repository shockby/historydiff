During World War II, the Kwantung Army Epidemic Prevention and Water Supply Department Headquarters, commonly known as Unit 731, was established on the outskirts of Harbin in Manchuria (present-day northeastern China).

In Japanese high school history textbooks, descriptions of Unit 731 are extremely limited. Many textbooks do not mention it directly, and even when they do, it tends to be a simple statement that ``research into bacterial weapons was being conducted.'' There is almost no mention of the specific details of the human experiments or the number of victims.

Meanwhile, in the field of academic research and reporting, it has been revealed that Unit 731, led by Lieutenant General Shiro Ishii, conducted inhumane human experiments on Chinese, Russian, and Korean prisoners of war (known as "Malta") in order to develop biological weapons such as plague, cholera, and anthrax. After the war, there continues to be debate as to why these acts were not brought to justice at the Tokyo Tribunal. The Japanese government officially acknowledges the existence and activities of the unit, but takes the position that the full story has not yet been elucidated, stating that ``no documentation remains'' regarding the details of the human experiments.
