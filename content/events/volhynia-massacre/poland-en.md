In Poland, the events that took place in the **Volhynia Region** in **1943-1944** are remembered as the ``Volhynian Massacre''**, a systematic massacre of the Polish population carried out by the **Ukrainian Uprising Army (UPA)**, the armed wing of the Organization of Ukrainian Nationalists (OUN). The Polish parliament has officially recognized this as ``genocide,'' and the number of victims is estimated to be between 50,000 and more than 100,000.

In history textbooks and government commentary, the UPA has been harshly criticized as a "criminal organization" that has killed civilians indiscriminately. The Polish side emphasizes that the massacre was not an inevitable "conflict" that occurred during Ukraine's independence struggle, but rather a unilateral ethnic cleansing aimed at eradicating the unarmed Polish community.

While the Polish government values ​​its security alliance with Ukraine, it believes it is extremely important to pursue historical truth and protect the dignity of victims. Specifically, we are strongly requesting that the Ukrainian government allow full-scale excavation, investigation, and official burial of the remains of Polish victims lying on Ukrainian territory. It also continues to publicly protest and express concerns about the heroism of the UPA and its leaders within Ukraine.
