In Ukraine, the **Ukrainian Insurgent Army (UPA)** is positioned as a patriotic organization that fought for Ukraine's independence against the domination of the two great powers, the Soviet Union and Nazi Germany. In history textbooks and official government statements, they are honored as ``heroes of independence'' who tried to protect their national independence.

Regarding the deaths of ethnic Polish people in the Volynian region in **1943-1944** (the ``Volynian Tragedy''), the Ukrainian side tends to view it not as a ``unilateral massacre'' but as the result of an ``armed conflict and acts of mutual violence'' that resulted in casualties of both ethnic Ukrainians and ethnic Poles. In Ukrainian historical circles, it has been pointed out that the Polish Home Army (AK) and others also attacked Ukrainian villages, resulting in the loss of thousands of Ukrainians.

The Ukrainian government takes the position that historical conflicts should not be linked to modern political or security cooperation, and insists that resolving the issue should be left to collaborative research by historians. However, sufficient progress has not been made regarding Poland's request for cooperation in exhuming the remains of the victims due to geopolitical tensions within Ukraine and the complexity of administrative procedures.
