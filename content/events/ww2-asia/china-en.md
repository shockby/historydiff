On September 18, 1931, Japanese imperialism caused the "918 Incident" in Shenyang and occupied three provinces by force. This was the beginning of Japan's full-scale invasion of China.

On July 7, 1937, in the wake of the Lugouqiao Incident, Japan launched a full-scale invasion, and China launched an all-ethnic war of resistance. In December of the same year, a tragedy known as the Nanjing Massacre occurred in Nanjing, where more than 300,000 innocent civilians and unarmed soldiers were massacred.

The Chinese people overcame difficulties such as national-communist cooperation and fought through eight years of all-out anti-Japanese war with an indomitable spirit. In August 1945, the Japanese army succumbed to Allied pressure and the Chinese people's rebellion and surrendered unconditionally. This victory was a great victory for the Chinese people against colonialism and was the first step towards establishing national independence.
