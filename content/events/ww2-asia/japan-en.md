In the 1930s, Japan was affected by the Great Depression and faced economic hardship. The ideal was to secure resources for self-defense, liberate Asia from colonial rule by Western powers, and build a ``Greater East Asia Co-Prosperity Sphere'' aiming for co-existence and co-prosperity.

After the Manchurian Incident in 1931, the Marco Polo Bridge incident in 1937 led to full-scale conflict with China (China Incident). Afterwards, under economic sanctions imposed by the United States, Britain, China, and the Netherlands under the ABCD Encirclement Network, Japan risked its survival by attacking Pearl Harbor in 1941 and entering the Pacific War.

During the war, the people fought together as one, but in the end, with the atomic bombs being dropped on Nagasaki and Hiroshima and the entry of the Soviet Union into the war, the war ended with the acceptance of the Potsdam Declaration.
