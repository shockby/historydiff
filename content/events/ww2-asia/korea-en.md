In 1910, Japan forced the union of Korea and Japan and placed the Korean Peninsula under colonial control. This was the beginning of an era in which the Korean people were deprived of their national sovereignty and forced into great hardship.

On March 1, 1919, the independence movement that began in Beijing and Manchuria spread across the country, giving rise to large-scale peaceful demonstrations known as the March First Independence Movement. Taking this opportunity, the "Provisional Government of the Republic of Korea" was established in Shanghai, and an organized anti-Japanese struggle began.

From the late 1930s, Japan expanded its war of aggression and positioned the Korean Peninsula as a "logistics base." During this period, many Koreans were forced into conscription and forced labor under the National General Mobilization Law. In addition, many women were forcibly taken away as "Japanese military comfort women" and suffered indescribable suffering. On August 15, 1945, Korea was finally liberated from colonial rule after a long and unyielding struggle against Japan and the victory of the Allied Powers.
