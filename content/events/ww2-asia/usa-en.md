In the 1930s, Japan tightened its military control and expanded its expansionist policies across Asia. The invasion of Manchuria in 1931 and the large-scale invasion of China that began in 1937 drew international condemnation.

To stop Japan's aggression, the United States tightened economic sanctions, particularly embargoing oil exports, and hampered Japan's ability to continue the war. In response, Japan launched a surprise attack on the U.S. naval base at Pearl Harbor, Hawaii, on December 7, 1941. This was the reason why America entered World War II on the side of the Allied Powers.

Initially, the Japanese army had the upper hand in the Philippines and Guam, but after victory at the Battle of Midway, the American army began a counterattack and gradually approached the Japanese mainland through island hopping (jumping tactics). The battle (World War II) finally ended with the dropping of the atomic bomb and Japan's total surrender.
